Healthy Food Suggestions

High-Protein

High protein foods are rich in a vital macronutrient essential for building and repairing tissues. They support muscle health and overall well-being.

  • Eggs (2 large):
    Versatile protein source with 12 grams per serving, assisting in muscle maintenance and overall health.
  • Greek Yogurt (6 oz):
    Packed with protein (around 15 grams) and probiotics, aiding in digestion and muscle recovery.
  • Salmon (3 oz):
    Rich in omega-3 fatty acids and offering around 21 grams of protein.

Fibre-Rich

Fiber-rich foods are packed with dietary fiber, promoting digestive health and providing a sense of fullness and satiety.

  • Black Beans (1/2 cup, cooked):
    High in fiber (7 grams), supporting digestion and satiety.
  • Broccoli (1 cup, cooked):
    Fiber-rich vegetable (5 grams) for digestive health.
  • Chia Seeds (2 tablespoons):
    Tiny seeds with 10 grams of fiber, promoting digestion and satiety.
Learn More

Low Calorie

Low-calorie foods have a reduced calorie content, making them ideal for weight management and calorie control.

  • Cucumber:
    Extremely low in calories, high in water content, and keeps you hydrated.
  • Watermelon:
    Refreshing and low-calorie, ideal for hydration, with approximately 46 calories per cup.
  • Cauliflower:
    Offers around 25 calories per cup, a great low-calorie substitute for starches.

Antioxidant Rich

Antioxidant-rich foods contain compounds that protect cells from free radical damage, supporting overall health and well-being.

  • Blueberries (1 cup):
    Loaded with antioxidants, they help protect your cells from damage.
  • Kale (1 cup, raw):
    Rich in vitamin C and beta-carotene, fighting inflammation and oxidative stress
  • Dark Chocolate (1 oz, 70% cocoa or higher):
    flavonoids that support heart health.

Weight loss

Weight loss foods aid in managing body weight and achieving weight loss goals, making them valuable for those seeking to lose weight.

  • Green Tea:
    Contains catechins that may boost metabolism and support weight loss.
  • Grapefruit:
    Known for its potential role in weight loss, it may help control appetite and regulate blood sugar.
  • Lentils:
    High in fiber and protein, promoting fullness and weight management.
Learn More

Energy-Boosting

Energy-boosting foods provide quick and sustained energy, combating fatigue and enhancing alertness.

  • Almonds (1 oz, about 23 almonds):
    Rich in healthy fats and protein for a quick energy boost.
  • Bananas (1 medium):
    Natural sugars like glucose, fructose, and sucrose provide quick energy.
  • Oatmeal (1/2 cup, dry):
    Complex carbs with soluble fiber for sustained energy.
